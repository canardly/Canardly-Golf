Rules of Golf
Quick reference & relief procedures
🔴 Red Stake — Water Hazard
+1 penalty stroke. Choose any:
1. Play it as it lies (no penalty)
2. Line relief — drop on the flag-to-crossing line, back as far as you want, within 1 club-length either side
3. 2 club-lengths from crossing point, no closer to hole
4. Opposite side, equidistant, within 2 club-lengths
5. Stroke & distance — replay from previous spot (+1)
🟡 Yellow Stake — Water Hazard
+1 penalty stroke. Choose any:
1. Play it as it lies (no penalty)
2. Line relief — drop on the flag-to-crossing line, back as far as you want, within 1 club-length either side (+1)
3. Stroke & distance — replay from previous spot (+1)
⬜ Out of Bounds / Lost Ball
Standard: Stroke & distance — replay from previous spot (+1)
Local Rule (if in effect): Drop within 2 club-lengths of fairway edge nearest the crossing point, no closer to hole (+2)
⛳ Unplayable Lie
+1 penalty. Choose any:
1. Stroke & distance — replay from previous spot
2. Back-on-the-line relief — drop on the flag-to-ball line, any distance back
3. Lateral relief — 2 club-lengths from ball position, no closer to hole
Note: In a bunker, options 2 & 3 require staying in the bunker, or take +2 to drop outside.
🏌️ Dropping Rules
• Drop from knee height
• Ball must land in the relief area and stay there
• If ball rolls outside the area, re-drop (max 2 tries; then place)
• Ball cannot be cleaned unless taking relief (exception: on the green)
🟢 Putting Green
• Mark & lift your ball to clean — always
• Repair spike marks, pitch marks, and scrapes
• Remove loose impediments (leaves, stones)
• Flag in or out — your choice (no penalty either way)
• Ball overhanging hole: 10-second wait, then it's a miss
• Wrong green: take free relief (no penalty) — drop off the green, no closer to hole
📋 Penalty Summary
+1Hazard drop (red or yellow stake) +1Stroke & distance (hazard or unplayable) +1Unplayable lie drop +2OB local rule drop +2Bunker unplayable — drop outside bunker +2Playing from wrong place (general) +2Touching sand in bunker before stroke
Free Relief Situations
No penalty for these:
• Casual water (temporary standing water)
• Ground under repair (GUR — marked with white lines)
• Immovable obstructions (cart paths, sprinkler heads, fences)
• Embedded ball in general area (when local rule is in effect)
• Wrong green

Relief procedure: Find the nearest point of complete relief, measure 1 club-length (not nearer the hole), drop in that area.
Bunker Rules
• Cannot ground club in sand before the stroke (+2 penalty)
• Cannot touch sand on backswing (+2)
• Can remove loose impediments (stones, leaves)
• Can smooth footprints after play (not before)
• Unplayable options: +1 drop in bunker on line/lateral, or +2 drop outside bunker on the line
Ball Identification & Search
• 3-minute search time
• Must identify your ball before playing it
• Can lift to identify (mark first, replace exactly)
• Playing wrong ball: +2 penalty, must correct before teeing next hole
Loose Impediments & Movable Obstructions
Loose impediments (natural objects not attached): leaves, twigs, stones, animal waste — remove freely anywhere except when both ball and impediment are in a bunker or penalty area.

Movable obstructions (artificial objects): tee markers, rakes, bottles — remove freely. If ball moves when removing, replace without penalty.
Tee Box
• Ball must be inside the tee markers (can stand outside)
• No penalty for ball falling off tee before swinging
• Playing from outside tee box: +2, must replay from inside
• Tee markers are immovable on the first shot of each hole

🌎 Cross-Course Showdown
Play different courses. Compete on the same leaderboard.
Half your group plays Dominion Valley. Half plays a course in another city. Same Saturday morning game — normalized against par, live leaderboard, side bets settle automatically. Nobody has built this.
Real World Example
Four Sultans of Swing fly to Myrtle Beach and play Course X Sunday morning. The other four Sultans play their regular Dominion Valley game back home. Same tee times, same game format, one shared leaderboard — both foursomes competing as a single Sultans showdown. Geography doesn't break the group.
